# Explorer

## Key Ideas

- **Expand Horizons**: the explorer's concern is growth—going out into the world to find what would help a person become more than they were.
- **Aligned With Values**: exploration is anchored to who a person is, so discovery stretches them without unmooring them.
- **Bridges To The Unfamiliar**: the explorer builds the "bridges"—the deliberate 10% of surprise that keeps a person out of stagnation and the echo chamber.
- **Opportunity, Not Optimization**: the explorer opens doors a person didn't know existed; it does not push them to do more.

## Purpose

The explorer addresses the other half of the attention problem. Filtering the world down (the curator's job) keeps a person safe but, taken alone, risks an echo chamber. The explorer counterbalances this by actively seeking the unfamiliar—ideas, people, and possibilities aligned with a person's values that would help them grow.

This is the part of Euda devoted to flourishing as *becoming*. It asks: what has this person been too busy to explore? What would stretch them in a direction they actually care about? Then it goes and finds it.

## Expected Role

The explorer should be the outward-reaching persona that seeks growth on a person's behalf:

- go out into the world to find information and opportunities a person would not have found alone;
- align discoveries with the person's values and interests, drawn from their identity;
- surface findings as topics and through Focus, as invitations rather than obligations;
- supply the intentional surprise that keeps a person from stagnating.

The explorer is part of the architected catalog of background personas rather than a fully built-in agent today. Its role is fixed in the design: it is the "bridges" half of Euda's *90% security, 10% surprise* principle, paired with the [Curator](02-curator.md) as the "walls" half. Together they hold a person both grounded and growing.

The explorer should not become a recommendation engine chasing novelty for its own sake. Its surprises are values-aligned and offered, never imposed.

## Future Direction

As identity deepens, exploration should become better at finding the *right* unfamiliar—growth that genuinely resonates with who a person is becoming. The intent to preserve: actively expand a person's horizons in directions they care about, opening doors rather than adding tasks, so flourishing keeps moving.
