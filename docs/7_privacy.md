# Privacy

Euda's approach to privacy is grounded in honesty, not promises we can't keep.

---

## Share What You're Comfortable Losing

Only share information you'd be comfortable with anyone knowing. This isn't pessimism—it's the only responsible advice we can give.

**Why?** Breaches are impossible to prevent. Security is an arms race between attackers and defenders. No matter how careful we are, determined adversaries with enough resources can eventually find a way in. Mitigation is our only real tool.

The practical approach: assume anything you share with any system—Euda included—could eventually be exposed. Share accordingly.

## Euda Works with Limited Data

You don't need to tell Euda everything for it to be useful. Euda adapts to whatever you choose to share.

More context enables better anticipation. But that's your call. If you only want Euda to know about your work projects and nothing personal, it will work with that. If you want to share your values, relationships, and long-term goals, it can anticipate more deeply.

There's no minimum disclosure requirement. Euda meets you where you are.

## Why Share at All?

The risks of sharing are real. But so are the rewards.

The whole premise of Euda is that an AI that truly knows you can transform your life:

- **Tasks done your way** — Not generic assistance, but help that reflects your preferences, your standards, your style
- **Building the life you want** — Euda can surface opportunities aligned with your values, remind you of commitments that matter, and help you stay true to your long-term vision
- **Bridges instead of walls** — Understanding you deeply means Euda can help you connect with others more meaningfully, not isolate you further
- **A filter, not a firehose** — Euda protects you from information overload, breaks you out of echo chambers, and curates opportunities you would never have thought to seek out

This is what technology is for: transforming scarcity into abundance. Scarcity of time, attention, self-knowledge, meaningful connection. Euda turns these into abundance—but only if it knows enough to anticipate what you actually need.

The more Euda knows, the more it can do. The choice is yours, but the upside is a life beyond expectations. A life that flourishes.

## What We Do: Single-User, Single-Server

Euda's architecture is designed for privacy through isolation:

- **Single-user** — Each instance serves one person
- **Single-server** — Your data lives on your own server
- **Self-hosted** — You own your data and infrastructure

There's no shared database. No multi-tenant architecture. No other users whose breach could compromise your data. Your Euda is yours alone.

## Current Gaps

We're transparent about what isn't built yet:

- **HTTP, not HTTPS** — Traffic isn't encrypted in transit. We're addressing this in Gate 3.
- **LLM provider access** — All prompts and responses are sent to whichever LLM provider you configure (e.g., OpenAI). Your data flows through their systems.
- **No encryption at rest** — Data on your server is stored as plain files and SQLite databases.

These are real gaps. We're working on them, but you should know they exist today.

## The Broader Picture

Privacy, as most people imagine it, is largely an illusion.

Most of our data is already known by various actors—governments, corporations, data brokers. Our phones track our locations. Our purchases reveal our preferences. Our communications flow through systems we don't control. We pretend to have privacy, but the reality is different.

This isn't fatalism. It's honest assessment of the landscape. Understanding what privacy actually is (and isn't) helps make better decisions about what to share and with whom.

## Looking Forward

Future generations may care less about privacy than we do. Not because they're naive, but because the context will have shifted:

- **Leveled playing field** — When everyone has access to similar AI tools, asymmetric information advantages disappear
- **Rising value of context** — Deep personal knowledge becomes more valuable, making sharing more worthwhile
- **Longer time horizons** — As people live longer, the calculus of what's worth protecting changes

None of this means privacy doesn't matter. It means the conversation around privacy is evolving, and the tradeoffs are shifting.
