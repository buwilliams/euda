How fast is AI improving?

Fermi calculations are new to me, not the concept of extrapolation, and I find them an interesting way to model the world. I hope you enjoy the model. Please let me know if you find any issues. - Year 2024: 1 week of work output in 1 hour with human + ai collaboration
- Year 2026: 1 month of work output in 1 hour with human + ai collaboration
- Year 2029: 1 week of work output by solo ai & 1 year of work output by human + ai collaboration
- Year 2032: 1 month of work output by solo ai

I need to take a moment to celebrate myself. Not a trait people love... but we do love a good underdog story.

I didn't finish high school or college. By high school, I realized school wasn't a place for exploration or learning, so I quit to forge a new path. I had a passion, a roaring fire, to understand "truth", if there was such a thing.

I knew I needed to gain a deeper understanding of reality, so I started by exploring the ancients. This pursuit has continued for over twenty-five hard years. Years of tirelessly swimming against the stream of societal expectations, refusing to be a cog in a wheel. Instead, thinking deeply and cultivating creativity to develop novel mental models on wide-ranging subjects, digging for every little bit of inspired thinking I could find. It's been an enormously lonely path. Just so many 4am studying sessions, filling numerous journals and loose-leaf papers, reading books that people often smirked at. Choosing remote work before it became trendy (and then fell out of favor) allowed me to take long walks, contemplate the latest domain I was exploring, and ignore the limiting beliefs of my peers/leaders.

Now we come to today, the intelligence age. In the AI economy, people like me are rare and highly valued. Currently, the powers that be are focused on AI researchers, but silently and unsung, are folks like me who leverage AI as a force multiplier, doing the work of entire departments/companies, but not just incrementally better, but step-change better, and mostly solo. AI has been a massive unlock for me.

Many of my technical/business peers are leveraging AI, but they have no idea what they are doing, seeing only 1.15 to 2x gains in productivity. So many know what they were taught to think, but not how to think. Unfortunately, they are ill-prepared for the current time, no expansive or creative thought. They are looking to everyone else for the answers. What's surprising to me is that they are asking all the wrong questions. Businesses must change drastically, but my peers seem unaware of anything beyond their technical expertise and political views.

I've had support and friends along the path who have been rocket boosters. No man is an island. With that said, it's a great time to be me because I was right to bet on myself all those years ago, a stroke of luck and serendipity.

When you read Sam's latest blog post, keep in mind the difference between AI capability and speculative application of capability. The difference helps to understand what AIs do vs. what that capability may produce.

The lines are blurry, but the capabilities are worth paying attention to:
- systems that are smarter than people in many ways=intelligence capability
- agents that can do real cognitive work=automation capability
- scientists... are two or three times more productive...tools... help us find further scientific insights=science discovery enablement capability
- a larval version of recursive self-improvement=recursive self-improvement capability

That's the big idea: AI that helps us improve AI.

I'm excited for the future!
