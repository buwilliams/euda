Buddy Williams, “AI Economics: The Time Traveler’s Gift” (Nov 09, 2025) argues that AI functions like “time travel” by collapsing decades of innovation into the present via intelligence/production efficiency (“the rate at which inputs are turned into outputs,” per Thomas Sowell). The piece claims AI will (1) raise total output by accelerating knowledge work, software, and scientific breakthroughs, and (2) lower prices broadly (example: TVs falling from ~$3,000 in 2000 to ~$200–$300 today; real TV costs down >99% since the 1960s). It also argues AI expands the “supply frontier” by enabling entirely new products/services, while automating cognitive labor and deflating traditional knowledge-work wages. The author anticipates shifts toward an “Outcome Economy” and redistribution mechanisms (e.g., universal income or equity in compute) to sustain dignity during transition. It concludes that as material scarcity fades, “meaning” (purpose/belonging) becomes the new scarce resource and key allocation problem.
