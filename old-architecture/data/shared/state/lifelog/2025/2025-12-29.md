That early independence fueled a lifelong habit of self-education. Rather than following a single academic or professional track, Buddy gravitated toward first principles: how systems behave under pressure, how incentives shape outcomes, and why well-intentioned structures often fail in practice. These questions led him into software development, entrepreneurship, and eventually AI—not as a fascination with tools, but as a study of what new tools make possible.

Across adulthood, his life has unfolded in parallel tracks: building a career in technology, sustaining long-term relationships, and becoming a parent—first to children born in the late 2000s and early 2010s, and later again as a father in his late thirties. Drawing from economics, systems theory, and critical rationalism, he focuses on how societies coordinate under conditions of uncertainty, dispersed knowledge, and imperfect incentives—especially as AI accelerates both creation and failure modes.
