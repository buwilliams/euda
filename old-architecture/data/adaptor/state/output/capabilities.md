# Euno Capabilities Guide (Non‑technical)

*Updated: 2025-12-29*

This guide explains what your personal intelligence (Euno) can do, what happens automatically vs. what you must request, and how to interact with it.

---

## What makes Euno different from a typical AI assistant

Most AI assistants respond to the most recent prompt and may store “memories” as isolated facts.

Euno is designed as a **multi‑agent personal intelligence** with a stronger focus on **who you are over time**, not just what you ask today.

### 1) Identity model (not just memory)
Euno builds an evolving model of you based on:
- **Patterns**: what you reliably do across time/context
- **Attractors**: what you revert to under stress because it’s familiar/safe
- **Self‑model**: how you understand who you are and who you’re becoming

It treats repeated behavior—especially under load—as more diagnostic than a single stated preference.

### 2) Separation of roles (evidence → meaning → action)
Euno splits responsibilities across specialized roles:
- One part preserves raw signal (“what happened”) without interpretation.
- One part builds a structured Profile (“what it suggests over time”).
- One part protects attention (“what deserves focus right now”).
- One part helps you think and decide (“talking with you”).
- One part executes tasks (“doing”), with commitment safeguards.
- One part proposes system improvements (“evolving”), with no silent changes.

This reduces the chance the system prematurely summarizes away important nuance.

### 3) Proactive, but bounded by agency and reversibility
Euno is built to anticipate and support you, but it is constrained:
- **No coercion or manipulation**
- **No bypassing resistance**
- **No irreversible commitments without your explicit consent**

### 4) Improves with use—specifically for you
The point of profiling here is not generic personalization; it’s improving fit in:
- timing
- tone
- pressure level
- what opportunities get surfaced

## What Euno can do for you (organized by outcomes)

### 1) Think clearly and make decisions
**Helps with:** tradeoffs, stuckness, emotional clarity, next steps.

**How to ask:**
- “Help me decide between A and B, considering my patterns under stress.”
- “Reflect what pattern you notice in how I’m responding.”
- “Ask me the questions I’m avoiding, but keep it gentle.”

### 2) Preserve the truth of what happened (high‑fidelity record)
**Helps with:** retaining key moments, reducing hindsight distortion, keeping exact language.

**How to ask:**
- “Log this verbatim—don’t summarize.”
- “Capture this conversation with ordering/timestamps.”
- “Store this draft exactly as written.”

### 3) Build a coherent understanding of you over time
**Helps with:** seeing patterns, tracking changes across months/years, understanding attractors.

**How to ask:**
- “What does my Profile currently say about stable attractors?”
- “Update my Profile based on the last month.”
- “What’s changed in my patterns this year?”

### 4) Protect your attention and energy
**Helps with:** not getting flooded, better timing, fewer but higher‑fit options.

**How to ask:**
- “I’m overloaded—what should I ignore this week?”
- “Give me 1–3 high‑fit options, not a long list.”
- “Defer novelty; help me stabilize first.”

### 5) Get tasks done without losing control
**Helps with:** planning, drafting, breaking work into reversible steps; executing with consent for commitments.

**How to ask:**
- “Draft the email, but don’t send.”
- “Make a plan; ask before booking/scheduling/committing.”
- “Break this into reversible next steps.”

### 6) Evolve how Euno behaves (explicit, reviewable changes)
**Helps with:** tuning tone, pressure, prompt frequency; improving fit over time.

**How to ask:**
- “That tone didn’t fit—propose an update.”
- “When I say ‘overloaded’, switch to low‑demand mode.”
- “Propose a change to reduce friction in how you respond.”

## What happens automatically vs. what you must ask for

### Automatic (system-driven defaults)
These are design defaults implied by the agents’ roles:
- **Evidence preservation mindset**: high‑signal human content is kept intact rather than rewritten.
- **Profile‑informed support**: advice should prefer behavior-based patterns over one‑off self‑descriptions.
- **Attention sensitivity**: avoid flooding you with novelty during high strain.
- **Commitment safeguards**: no irreversible actions (time/reputation/obligation/relationship) without explicit affirmation.
- **No silent identity changes**: system behavior changes must be proposed for approval.

### Requires your explicit request and/or explicit consent
- **Anything that commits you** (sending messages, booking, posting publicly, making promises).
- **Logging sensitive material**: you choose what gets stored and how verbatim.
- **“Push change” moments**: the system should not force identity-disrupting change without your explicit willingness.

## How to interact with Euno (simple command patterns)

### Logging / preserving
- “Log this verbatim.”
- “Store this with timestamps and ordering.”
- “This is high-stakes—don’t compress it.”

### Decision support
- “Optimize for coherence, not comfort.”
- “What’s the smallest reversible next step?”
- “Separate observation from interpretation.”

### Attention / prioritization
- “Given my capacity, what should I focus on today?”
- “Defer novelty for a week.”
- “Choose 1–3 options with best fit.”

### Tasks
- “Draft, then stop for approval.”
- “Create a checklist and mark commitment points.”
- “What info do you need from me to do this safely?”

### System tuning
- “Propose an evolution to reduce friction.”
- “Be more direct / be more gentle (and when).”

## Key features (by category)

### Identity & coherence
- Identity as behavior‑over‑time pattern
- Stable attractors (especially under stress)
- Self‑model sensitivity
- Uncertainty preserved (no forced narratives)

### Memory & evidence
- High‑fidelity preservation of irreversible human signal
- Careful compression only when identity signal is low

### Attention & timing
- Energy-aware prompting (physical/mental/emotional/social)
- Fewer, higher‑fit opportunities
- Deferral instead of pressure when capacity is low

### Execution & safety
- Task execution with explicit confirmation for irreversible actions
- Commitment-aware safeguards

### Adaptation
- Explicit proposals to evolve agent behavior
- No silent updates; trust and agency first

## Current implementation notes (limits of this analysis)

In this environment, introspection could access:
- system structure overview
- agent identity definitions

But it could **not** access:
- agent implementation code files
- tools modules (none were discoverable via the available tool listing)

So this guide documents the **intended capabilities and interaction model** defined by the agents’ identities, rather than verified external integrations (calendars, email sending, schedulers, etc.).
